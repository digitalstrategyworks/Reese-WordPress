When Sharks Attack...

Summer vacation for many people means lemonade, lawn chairs, sandals and beaches. For many, this also means the possibility of shark attacks. The International Shark Attack File (ISAF) reported 115 shark attacks worldwide in 2010, 79 of which were unprovoked. This number of unprovoked shark attacks has steadily risen each decade-â€”a growth that the ISAF attributes to higher populations of humans spending more time in the ocean.

Although the chances of getting attacked by a shark are rare (where the probability is determined by factors such as location and activity), it is important to be informed before jumping into the ocean. This presentation provides information about the methods a shark uses to hunt prey, how to prevent shark attacks and what to do in the event of an attack.
