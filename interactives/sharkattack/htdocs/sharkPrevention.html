Prevention Methods

    In order to reduce the chances of you becoming shark food, it is crucial that you take the necessary preventative measures to deter sharks from attacking you. Remember that you put yourself at risk for a shark attack whenever you enter the ocean, no matter how shallow the water is.
  • Look out for signs indicating that sharks have been sighted or attacks reported. Ignoring these markers means swimming at your own risk.
  • Swim in a group when at the beach. Individuals swimming alone may appear as wounded prey to sharks. In the event of an actual attack, groups have higher chances of survival and are more likely to make it to shore alive.
  • Avoid swimming during dawn, dusk and night. Many species of shark feed during these times, and may move towards the shoreline to hunt for fish.
  • Try not to splash excessively when swimming in the ocean. Sharks interpret flailing and wild splashing as injured prey.
